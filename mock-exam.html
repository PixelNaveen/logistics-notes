MTL1062 Introduction to Logistics Management

Mock Examination Paper 2024/2025

Time Allowed: 3 Hours | Total Marks: 100

SECTION A (COMPULSORY) - 40 Marks

Answer ALL parts. Write answers clearly and concisely.

Question 1 (40 marks total)

a) [TOPIC: Fundamentals] Define "Logistics Management" and explain how it differs from "Supply Chain Management" in terms of scope and organizational involvement. (8 marks)

b) [TOPIC: 7 R's Concept] List and briefly explain the 7 R's of Logistics Management, providing one practical example for each 'R'. (7 marks)

c) [TOPIC: Supply Chain Flows] Identify and describe the three main flows in a supply chain network, explaining the direction and purpose of each flow. (6 marks)

d) [TOPIC: Inventory Types] Name the five types of inventory found in a manufacturing organization and provide one example of each type. (5 marks)

e) [TOPIC: Bullwhip Effect] Explain what causes the "Bullwhip Effect" in supply chains and suggest two methods to reduce its impact. (4 marks)

f) [TOPIC: Push vs Pull] Compare Push and Pull production strategies in terms of their driving forces and inventory implications. (4 marks)

g) [TOPIC: Transportation] State three factors that influence the selection of transportation modes for international shipping. (3 marks)

h) [TOPIC: Humanitarian Logistics] Explain why logistics is considered "80% of humanitarian operations" and name two key challenges faced in humanitarian logistics. (3 marks)

SECTION B - 60 Marks

Answer any THREE questions from the following. Each question carries 20 marks.

Question 2 [TOPIC: Inventory Management & EOQ]

a) "The objective of Inventory Management is to strike a balance between inventory investment and customer service level." Explain this statement with appropriate examples. (6 marks)

b) Explain the Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) model with its underlying assumptions. Draw a graph showing the relationship between ordering costs, holding costs, and total costs. (8 marks)

c) CALCULATION PROBLEM:

Marine Logistics Ltd. purchases navigation equipment with the following data:

  • Annual demand: 1,800 units
  • Ordering cost: $150 per order
  • Unit cost: $800
  • Annual holding cost rate: 15% of unit cost

Calculate:

  1. EOQ
  2. Total annual cost at EOQ
  3. Number of orders per year
  4. Time between orders (assume 250 working days)

Question 3 [TOPIC: 3PL/4PL & Outsourcing]

a) List and briefly explain five key functions that a Third-Party Logistics (3PL) provider typically offers to its clients. (10 marks)

b) Compare and contrast 3PL and 4PL service providers in terms of: scope of operations, strategic involvement, asset ownership, client relationships. (6 marks)

c) Describe the step-by-step process a company should follow when selecting a 3PL service provider, from initial identification to final contract negotiation. (4 marks)

Question 4 [TOPIC: JIT/Lean Systems & Waste Management]

a) Define "Lean Manufacturing" and explain its three fundamental pillars. (6 marks)

b) Identify and explain the eight types of waste (TIMWOOD + 1) that can occur in a hospital environment, providing specific healthcare examples for each waste type. (10 marks)

c) Explain how the "Pull Strategy" contributes to waste reduction in manufacturing operations, using real-world examples. (4 marks)

Question 5 [TOPIC: Warehouse Management]

a) Compare and contrast a "Warehouse" and a "Distribution Center" in terms of: primary purpose, storage duration, technology requirements, customer interaction. (8 marks)

b) Draw a flowchart showing the main warehouse functions from goods receipt to dispatch, and briefly explain each step. (8 marks)

c) List four key factors that should be considered when designing an efficient warehouse layout. (4 marks)

Question 6 [TOPIC: Reverse Logistics & Sustainability]

a) Define "Reverse Logistics" and explain how it differs from traditional forward logistics operations. (5 marks)

b) Explain the relationship between "Green Logistics" and "Reverse Logistics," discussing how reverse logistics contributes to environmental sustainability. (7 marks)

c) Identify and explain four drivers and three constraints that companies face when implementing reverse logistics practices, particularly in developing economies like Sri Lanka. (8 marks)

Question 7 [TOPIC: Transportation & Intermodal Systems]

a) Compare the four main modes of international transportation (Air, Road, Rail, Water) in terms of: Speed, Cost, Capacity, Flexibility. Create a comparison table with examples of suitable cargo for each mode. (12 marks)

b) Explain the concept of "Intermodal Transportation" and discuss three benefits it offers to international logistics operations. (5 marks)

c) Discuss the role of containerization in modern global supply chains, mentioning both advantages and challenges. (3 marks)

Question 8 [TOPIC: Contemporary Issues & Future Trends]

a) Identify and discuss four major contemporary challenges facing logistics operations in the post-COVID global environment. (12 marks)

b) Explain the concept of "Logistics Performance Index (LPI)" and discuss why it is important for countries to benchmark their logistics performance. (5 marks)

c) Describe two emerging technologies that are transforming modern logistics operations, providing specific examples of their applications. (3 marks)

ANSWER SCHEME GUIDE

Section A Expected Answers:

1a) Logistics vs SCM:

  • Logistics: Planning, implementing, controlling flow within organization
  • SCM: Managing entire network across multiple organizations
  • Scope: Single vs multiple organizations
  • Focus: Operational vs strategic

1b) 7 R's: Right Product, Quantity, Condition, Place, Time, Customer, Price with examples

1c) Three Flows: Product flow (forward/reverse), Information flow (demand, orders, tracking), Financial flow (payments, credits)

1d) Five Inventory Types: Raw materials, WIP, Finished goods, Pipeline, MRO

1e) Bullwhip Effect: Information failure, demand amplification upstream; Solutions: Information sharing, CPFR

1f) Push vs Pull: Forecast-driven vs demand-driven; Higher vs lower inventory

1g) Transportation Factors: Distance, urgency, cost, product nature, infrastructure

1h) Humanitarian 80%: Most operations are logistics; Challenges: Infrastructure, security, coordination

Section B High-Scoring Answer Elements:

  • Question 2: Balance concept, EOQ graph, correct calculation steps
  • Question 3: Complete 3PL functions, clear 3PL vs 4PL table, selection process steps
  • Question 4: Lean pillars, all 8 wastes with hospital examples, pull strategy benefits
  • Question 5: Clear warehouse vs DC comparison, complete flowchart, design factors
  • Question 6: Reverse definition, green logistics relationship, drivers/constraints with examples
  • Question 7: Complete mode comparison table, intermodal definition/benefits, containerization
  • Question 8: Post-COVID challenges, LPI importance, emerging technologies
